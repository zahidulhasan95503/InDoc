How to Apply for Passport Online & Offline

A Passport is an official government-issued travel document that certifies the identity and nationality of its holder for the purpose of international travel. In India, passports are issued by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) through the Central Passport Organization (CPO) and its network of Passport Seva Kendras (PSKs) and Post Office Passport Seva Kendras (POPSKs) across the country.

An Indian passport contains the holder's name, photograph, date of birth, place of birth, gender, nationality, and a unique Passport Number. It is one of the most important identity and travel documents for any Indian citizen. There are three types of Indian passports:

  • Ordinary Passport (Type P) — Blue cover, issued to regular citizens for leisure/business travel
  • Official Passport (Type S) — White cover, issued to government officials for official travel
  • Diplomatic Passport (Type D) — Maroon cover, issued to diplomats and senior government officials
Important to Know
  • A passport is mandatory for travelling to any foreign country
  • The application fee is ₹1,500 for a normal (36-page) passport and ₹2,000 for a jumbo (60-page) passport
  • Tatkal (urgent) fee is an additional ₹2,000 on top of the normal fee
  • You must apply through the official Passport Seva Portal — www.passportindia.gov.in
  • After online submission, a mandatory appointment at PSK/POPSK is required for document verification
  • Police verification will be conducted at your residential address after PSK visit
  • Normal processing takes 30-45 days; Tatkal takes 1-3 working days (after PSK visit)
  • Passport is valid for 10 years for adults (18+) and 5 years for minors (below 18)

Documents Required for Passport Application

Note: You must carry original documents along with self-attested photocopies to the Passport Seva Kendra. Aadhaar Card is now mandatory for all passport applications.
Proof of Date of Birth (any one)
  • Birth Certificate issued by the Municipal Authority / Registrar of Births & Deaths
  • Transfer / School Leaving Certificate (with DOB mentioned)
  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card / Aadhaar Letter
  • Voter ID Card (EPIC)
  • Driving License
  • Policy Bond issued by Public Life Insurance Corporations / Companies with DOB
  • Affidavit from Executive Magistrate / First Class Judicial Magistrate (if no other document)
Proof of Present Address (any one)
  • Aadhaar Card (preferred — linked with address)
  • Electricity Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Water Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Telephone / Landline Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Bank Account Statement / Passbook (recent)
  • Voter ID Card
  • Income Tax Assessment Order
  • Gas Connection Proof (Piped Gas bill in applicant's name)
  • Registered Rent Agreement
  • Spouse's Passport (for women — first and last page copy with address)
  • Parent's Passport (for minors — first and last page copy)
For Re-issue / Renewal
  • Old Passport (original — mandatory)
  • Self-attested photocopy of first 2 and last 2 pages of old passport
  • Self-attested photocopy of ECR / Non-ECR page
  • Self-attested photocopy of validity page and observation page (if any)
  • All previous passports (if applicable)
  • If name changed: Legal document (e.g., Gazette Notification, Marriage Certificate, Deed Poll)
  • If address changed: New address proof
Special Cases
  • Minor (below 18): Birth Certificate + Parents' passports + Aadhaar of parents. Consent of both parents required (Annexure D)
  • Government Employees: NOC (No Objection Certificate) from the employer or prior intimation letter / Identity Certificate
  • Tatkal Applications: Additional verification annexures (Annexure F — verification certificate from employer/educational institution/known person)
  • Lost / Damaged Passport: FIR copy + old passport details + affidavit
  • Senior Citizens (60+): May get priority tokens and expedited processing at some PSKs

How to Apply for Passport Online (via Passport Seva Portal)

The Passport Seva Portal is the official online platform by the Ministry of External Affairs for all passport-related services. You can fill the application form, pay the fee, and book an appointment at the nearest Passport Seva Kendra — all from the comfort of your home.

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Visit the Passport Seva Portal
    Go to www.passportindia.gov.in — the official Passport Seva website by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India.
  2. Register as a New User
    If you are a first-time user:
    • Click on "Register Now" on the homepage
    • Select the Passport Office nearest to your residential address
    • Enter your Full Name, Date of Birth, Email ID, and Login ID (create a username)
    • Set a Password and choose a Hint Question
    • Enter the CAPTCHA and click "Register"
    • You will receive an activation link on your email — click it to activate your account
    If already registered, simply Login with your Login ID and password.
  3. Click on "Apply for Fresh Passport / Re-issue of Passport"
    After logging in, go to the Dashboard and click on:
    • "Apply for Fresh Passport" — if applying for the first time
    • "Re-issue of Passport" — if renewing, replacing a damaged/lost passport, or making changes (name, address, etc.)
    Select "Click here to fill the application form online" to proceed.
  4. Fill the Passport Application Form
    Complete the application form with the following details across multiple sections:
    • Type of Application — Fresh / Re-issue
    • Type of Passport Booklet — 36 pages (Normal) or 60 pages (Jumbo)
    • Applicant's Name — Given Name, Surname (as per supporting documents)
    • Date of Birth and Place of Birth
    • Gender and Marital Status
    • Father's / Mother's / Legal Guardian's Name
    • Present Residential Address (with PIN code)
    • Permanent Address (can be same as present address)
    • Emergency Contact Details
    • Identity Certificate / Aadhaar Number
    • Previous Passport Details (if Re-issue)
  5. Upload Supporting Documents (if applicable)
    For certain services (e.g., Tatkal, Re-issue), you may need to upload:
    • Scanned copy of old passport (first and last pages)
    • Scanned copy of self-attested supporting documents
    • Aadhaar card (front and back)
    Note: For a normal fresh passport, document upload may not be required at this stage — you will present originals at the PSK.
  6. Preview and Submit Application
    Review all the details you entered carefully. Once satisfied, check the declaration and click "Submit".
    After submission, you will receive an Application Reference Number (ARN). SAVE THIS NUMBER! You'll need it for payment, appointment booking, and tracking.
  7. Pay the Passport Fee Online
    After submission, go to "Pay and Schedule Appointment". Payment options include:
    • Credit Card / Debit Card (Visa, MasterCard, RuPay)
    • Net Banking (SBI and associate banks)
    • UPI
    • Challan — Pay at SBI branch (valid for 3 days)
    Fee Structure:
    Service Normal Fee Tatkal Fee (additional)
    Fresh Passport (36 pages) ₹1,500 +₹2,000
    Fresh Passport (60 pages / Jumbo) ₹2,000 +₹2,000
    Re-issue (36 pages) ₹1,500 +₹2,000
    Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) ₹500 N/A
  8. Schedule an Appointment at PSK / POPSK
    After payment, you will be directed to book an appointment:
    • Select the Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK) nearest to you
    • Choose an available Date and Time Slot
    • Confirm and print the appointment confirmation
  9. Visit the PSK / POPSK on Appointment Date
    On the day of your appointment, visit the PSK/POPSK with:
    • Printed Application Receipt with ARN and appointment details
    • All original documents (address proof, age proof, ID proof)
    • Self-attested photocopies of all documents
    • Aadhaar Card (original + photocopy)
    At the PSK, you will go through:
    • Counter A — Token and document submission
    • Counter B — Document verification by Passport Officer
    • Counter C — Biometrics (photo, fingerprints, digital signature)
    • Counter D — Final verification and Granting Officer approval
  10. Police Verification
    After the PSK visit, police verification will be initiated at your residential address:
    • A Police Officer will visit your home to verify your identity and address
    • Keep your original documents and passport application receipt ready
    • For Tatkal applications, police verification may happen after passport dispatch
    • For Normal applications, passport is dispatched after positive police verification
  11. Receive Your Passport
    Once the police verification is cleared and your application is approved:
    • The passport is printed at the Central Passport Printing Facility
    • It is dispatched to your registered address via Speed Post
    • You will receive an SMS notification with the Speed Post tracking number
    • Normal processing: 30-45 days from PSK visit
    • Tatkal processing: 1-3 working days from PSK visit
Note: Save your Application Reference Number (ARN) and File Number safely. You can track your application status at Track Application Status.

Apply via mPassport Seva Mobile App

The mPassport Seva App is the official mobile application by the Ministry of External Affairs. It is available on both Android and iOS. The app provides the same functionality as the Passport Seva Portal, optimised for mobile devices.

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Download the mPassport Seva App
    Google Play Store (Android)
    Apple App Store (iOS)
  2. Register & Login
    Open the app → Create a new account or login with your existing Passport Seva Portal credentials (same account works across both platforms).
  3. Select "Apply for Fresh Passport" or "Re-issue"
    From the app dashboard, select the appropriate service and fill the application form (same fields as the web portal).
  4. Pay Fee & Book Appointment
    Pay using available online methods (Credit/Debit Card, Net Banking, UPI) and book your PSK/POPSK appointment.
  5. Visit PSK with Documents
    Visit the PSK on the appointment date with all original documents for verification and biometric capture.
  6. Track Application
    Track your passport application status directly in the app using your ARN or File Number.
Tip: The mPassport Seva app also lets you locate nearby PSK/POPSKs, check fee structure, download forms, and get real-time application status updates.

How to Apply for Passport Offline (Visit Passport Seva Kendra)

While the Government of India strongly recommends using the online portal, you can still apply for a passport by visiting a Passport Seva Kendra (PSK), Regional Passport Office (RPO), or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK). However, even for the offline process, you must fill the application form online on the Passport Seva Portal — the form cannot be submitted manually on paper anymore.

Important Note: As of current government guidelines, there is no fully offline paper-based application process for passports. The application form must be filled online at passportindia.gov.in. However, you can get assistance at the PSK or authorised centres. Here is how the process works:
Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Get Help at a Common Service Centre (CSC) or Internet Café
    If you are unable to fill the form online yourself, visit a:
    • Common Service Centre (CSC) — Government-authorised centres available across India. Find one at www.csc.gov.in
    • Internet café or authorised agent
    • Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK) — Many post offices assist in form filling
    They will help you fill the online form, pay the fee, and book an appointment on your behalf.
  2. Gather All Required Documents
    Prepare the following documents before your PSK visit:
    • Printed Application Receipt (with ARN and barcode)
    • Proof of Date of Birth — Birth Certificate, School Leaving Certificate, 10th marksheet, etc.
    • Proof of Present Address — Aadhaar, Electricity bill, Water bill, Telephone bill, Bank statement, Rent agreement, etc.
    • Aadhaar Card (mandatory — original + photocopy)
    • PAN Card (optional but recommended)
    • Old Passport (for re-issue applications)
    • 2 Passport-sized Photographs (white background, recent)
    Carry both originals and self-attested photocopies of all documents.
  3. Visit the PSK / POPSK on Your Appointment Date
    Arrive at the Passport Seva Kendra at least 15 minutes before your appointment time. The process at PSK involves:
    • Queue Management — Token generation at the entrance
    • Counter A — Form and document submission
    • Counter B — Document verification by the Passport Granting Officer
    • Counter C — Biometric data capture (photograph, fingerprints, digital signature)
    • Counter D — Final verification and file completion
    The entire PSK process usually takes 2-4 hours.
  4. Police Verification
    Police verification will be initiated after your PSK visit:
    • A local Police Officer will visit your residential address
    • Verify your identity, address, and personal details
    • You may be asked to visit the local Police Station in some cases
    Tip: Keep your application receipt and original documents ready at all times until police verification is complete.
  5. Passport Dispatch via Speed Post
    After successful police verification and application approval:
    • The passport is printed and dispatched via India Post Speed Post
    • You will receive an SMS notification with the tracking number
    • Delivery typically takes 3-7 working days after dispatch
Processing Time: Normal passport processing takes 30-45 days from PSK visit. Tatkal passport is dispatched within 1-3 working days of the PSK visit (police verification may be conducted post-dispatch for Tatkal).

After Application — What Next?

Track Application Status

Visit Track Status on Passport Seva Portal
Login → Go to "Track Application Status" → Enter your File Number to check the current status including police verification, printing, and dispatch.

Track Speed Post Delivery

Visit India Post Speed Post Tracking
Once your passport is dispatched, track your Speed Post delivery using the tracking number received via SMS.

Check Police Verification Status

Visit Passport Seva Portal
Login → Track Application Status → Your police verification status will be shown along with the name and contact of the verifying officer.

Download e-Passport / Appointment Receipt

Visit Passport Seva Portal Login
Login and download your appointment receipt, application receipt (with ARN), and check if your passport has been dispatched.

Types of Passport Services

Fresh Passport — First-time passport application (Normal & Tatkal)
Re-issue of Passport — Renewal, name/address change, damaged, lost, additional pages
Police Clearance Certificate (PCC) — Required for visa/immigration of certain countries
Identity Certificate — Issued to non-Indian nationals in India
Emergency Certificate (EC) — Issued to Indian nationals abroad for emergency one-time travel
Diplomatic / Official Passport — For government officials on official duty abroad

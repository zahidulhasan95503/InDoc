Common Issues

Mismatches between the application and Aadhaar/supporting documents — such as variations in name spelling (e.g., "Rahul K. Singh" vs. "Rahul Kumar Singh"), Date of Birth, or address — are the primary cause of PAN application rejection.

How to fix:

  • Cross-verify Details: Before applying, ensure your name, DOB, and address exactly match your Aadhaar card and other supporting documents.
  • Apply / Correct Online: Submit a new application or correction request via the NSDL PAN Portal or UTIITSL Portal.

Tip: Use the exact name format as printed on your Aadhaar — avoid abbreviations or initials unless they appear on the original document.

Submission of blurred, illegible, or non-compliant photo/scanned documents leads to immediate rejection. For physical applications, use original photographs — not scanned copies. Ensure documents are clear, recent, and in the prescribed format.

How to fix:

  • Photo Specifications: Use a recent colour photograph (3.5 cm × 2.5 cm) with a white background. The face should cover about 80% of the frame.
  • Document Quality: Ensure all uploaded documents are clearly readable, not cropped, and within the file-size limit specified on the portal.
  • Review Guidelines: Check the official PAN Application Guidelines – Income Tax Portal for the full list of acceptable documents.

Signatures that do not match other ID proofs cause application rejection. Using blue ink instead of black ink is another common reason for problems with physical PAN applications.

How to fix:

  • Use Black Ink: Always sign physical PAN application forms with black ink only.
  • Maintain Consistency: Ensure your signature matches the one on your existing identity documents (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, etc.).
  • For Minors / Illiterate Applicants: A thumb impression or representative's signature is acceptable — refer to the Form 49A instructions for details.

Missing or incorrect PIN codes in the application form prevent successful processing, as the physical PAN card is delivered by India Post to the address mentioned in the application.

How to fix:

  • Verify PIN Code: Double-check your 6-digit PIN code before submitting. Use the India Post PIN Code Finder to confirm the correct code for your area.
  • Correct Address Online: If your PAN already has an incorrect address, submit a correction request via the NSDL PAN Correction Portal.

Note: The address on PAN need not match Aadhaar, but it must be a valid, deliverable postal address with the correct PIN code.

Unintentionally applying for a new PAN when one already exists is illegal and causes issues with the Income Tax department. Holding more than one PAN can attract a penalty of ₹10,000 under Section 272B of the Income Tax Act.

How to fix:

  • Check Existing PAN: Verify if you already have a PAN at the Know Your PAN – Income Tax Portal.
  • Surrender Duplicate: Submit a request to surrender the duplicate PAN via the NSDL PAN Portal. Mention the PAN to be retained and the one to be cancelled in the correction form.

Important: Every individual / entity is legally allowed to hold only one PAN. Surrender the extra PAN immediately to avoid penalties.

If documentation or data is incomplete, applications often get stuck in an "Under Process" status for weeks, forcing applicants to submit correction forms to resolve the issue.

How to fix:

  • Track Status: Check your application status using your 15-digit acknowledgment number at the NSDL PAN Status Tracking Portal.
  • Submit Correction Form: If the application is stuck, submit Form 49A (Indian citizens) or Form 49AA (foreign citizens) with the correct information via the NSDL Correction Portal.
  • Contact Support: If the status does not change for more than 30 days, contact the NSDL TIN Call Centre at 020-27218080 or email tininfo@nsdl.co.in.

Omitting crucial information — such as initials in the name (you must use your full name as per documents), or not providing proper Father's Name or complete address details — leads to rejection.

How to fix:

  • Use Full Name: Enter your complete name without abbreviations. For example, write "Rajesh Kumar Sharma" instead of "R. K. Sharma".
  • Father's Name: Provide the father's full name as it appears on official records. This is mandatory for all individual applicants.
  • Correct via NSDL: If your PAN already has incorrect details, submit a correction at the NSDL PAN Correction / Change Request Portal.

Tip: Fill every mandatory field carefully and review the form before submission.

Any mismatch between Aadhaar and PAN data prevents seamless linking, which is mandatory under the Income Tax Act. An unlinked PAN becomes inoperative, blocking ITR filing, refunds, and financial transactions.

How to fix:

  • Link Aadhaar-PAN: Link your Aadhaar with PAN at the Income Tax Aadhaar-PAN Linking Portal.
  • Check Linking Status: Verify if your Aadhaar and PAN are already linked at the Aadhaar-PAN Link Status Page.
  • Fix Data Mismatches First: If names or DOB differ between Aadhaar and PAN, correct them in either document before attempting to link. Use the NSDL PAN Correction Portal or the UIDAI Aadhaar Update Portal.

Important: The deadline for linking has been extended multiple times — check the Income Tax portal for the latest deadline. A fee of ₹1,000 may apply for late linking.

How to get Birth Certificate

A Birth Certificate is a vital administrative document issued by the Government of India that records the birth of a child. It serves as permanent proof of identity, age, and nationality.

Registering a birth is mandatory in India under the Registration of Births and Deaths (RBD) Act, 1969. A birth certificate is required for various fundamental rights and services, including school admissions, obtaining a passport, voter ID, driving license, and Aadhaar card.

Official Resource Link: National Portal of India - Obtain Birth Certificate

Important to Know
  • Birth must be registered within 21 days of the event, totally FREE of cost.
  • In institutional births (hospitals/nursing homes), the medical institution is responsible for reporting to the Registrar.
  • In domiciliary (home) births, the head of the household must report it.
  • Delayed registration (after 21 days) attracts late fees and requires additional affidavits or magistrate permission.

Documents Required for Birth Certificate

Note: Keep original documents with you for verification at the Registrar's office.
Institutional Births (Hospital)
  • Proof of birth / Discharge letter issued by the hospital where the child was born.
  • Parents' Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN Card, or Passport).
  • Parents' Address Proof (Aadhaar, utility bills, etc.).
  • Marriage Certificate of parents (optional but recommended in some states).
Domiciliary Births (Home)
  • Affidavit from the parents detailing the birth date, time, and location.
  • Letter/certificate from the person/midwife who assisted in the delivery.
  • Identity and Address Proof of parents.
  • Declaration by head of family or nearest relative present at birth.

How to Apply for Birth Certificate Online

The Government of India provides the Civil Registration System (CRS) portal for online birth registration in supported States and Union Territories. Some states also have their own dedicated municipal portals.

Official Online Portal Link: Civil Registration System (crsorgi.gov.in)

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Visit the Official CRS Portal
    Go to crsorgi.gov.in — the official Civil Registration System portal.
  2. General Public Sign Up / Login
    If you are a new user, click on "General Public Sign Up" to create an account. You will need to provide your username, email ID, and mobile number.
    • Select your State, District, Subdistrict, and Village/Town.
    • Verify using OTP sent to your registered mobile number.
    • If already registered, log in using your credentials.
  3. Fill the Online Birth Registration Form
    After logging in, navigate to the "Add Birth Registration" section. Complete the online form carefully with these details:
    • Child's Details: Name (if decided), Date of Birth, Gender.
    • Place of Birth: Hospital Name or Home Address.
    • Parents' Details: Father's and Mother's names, Aadhaar numbers, and occupations.
    • Address: Current address of parents at the time of birth and permanent address.
  4. Upload Required Documents
    Upload scanned copies of required documents in the specified format (usually PDF/JPEG). This includes identity proof of parents and hospital letters.
  5. Submit and Print Application
    Review all details thoroughly. Click "Submit" to complete your application.
    Take a printout of the submitted application form which will bear a reference or registration number.
  6. Visit Registrar Office
    Important: You must visit the local Registrar's office with the printed application form and original documents for physical verification within the stipulated time for the certificate to be approved.
State Portals: If your state is not fully covered by CRS, you may need to apply through your state's specific e-District or Municipal Corporation website (e.g., e-District Delhi, e-Sevai TN, BMC portal Mumbai).

How to Apply for Birth Certificate offline

If you prefer the offline method or if online registration isn't available in your region, you can apply physically at your local municipal or panchayat office.

Official Resource Link: India.gov.in - How to Obtain Birth Certificate

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Obtain the Registration Form
    Get the birth registration form from the local Municipal Corporation, Nagar Palika, or Gram Panchayat office.
  2. Collect Hospital Letter (for institutional births)
    If the birth occurred in a hospital, collect the discharge summary or the letter provided by the Medical Officer in charge, confirming the birth.
  3. Fill the Application Form
    Fill in the required information, including child's date of birth, gender, and parents' details. Make sure no spelling mistakes are made as correction later is difficult.
  4. Attach Documents
    Attach self-attested photocopies of identity proof and address proof of the parents, along with the hospital discharge summary (if applicable) and a marriage certificate.
  5. Submit the Application
    Submit the completed application form and documents to the Registrar of Births and Deaths at the local municipal or panchayat authority.
  6. Physical Verification
    The authorities verify the application and documents. For home births, a physical verification of the address might be conducted by a local officer.
  7. Collect Certificate
    Once verification is complete, the Birth Certificate is issued. You can collect the physical copy from the office after the designated processing time (usually 7-15 days).

Delayed Birth Registration Rules

If a birth is not registered within 21 days, delayed registration rules apply under the RBD Act:

Delay Period Required Action & Authority Late Fee
21 to 30 Days Written permission of the Registrar. Nominal late fee (e.g., ₹2 - ₹10 depending on state).
30 Days to 1 Year Written permission of the prescribed authority (usually Chief Medical Officer or Sub-Divisional Magistrate) and production of an affidavit made before a notary public. Late fee applicable (e.g., ₹5 - ₹50).
More than 1 Year Registration can only be made on an order made by a Magistrate of the First Class or Presidency Magistrate after verifying the correctness of the birth. Obtain a Non-Availability Certificate (NAC) first. Higher late fee applicable (e.g., ₹10 - ₹100+).

How to apply for VOTER Id CARD Online & Offline!


The Election Commission ID is also known as the Voter's ID in India. It is an important document that Indian citizens need in order to be 
able to vote.Applying for a voter ID online has become a convenient method for many. The Voter ID card, a crucial document for Indian citizens to 
participate in elections, can be obtained both online and offline.


Basic Requirements to Apply for Voter ID Card Online

Age: You've got to be 18 or older. No exceptions.

Citizenship: Indian citizen is the keyword here. Sorry, United States or any other country, this isn't for you.

Mental Health: A court-declared mental incapacity disqualifies an individual.

Special Cases

Non-Resident Indians (NRIs): Form 6A is available for application.

Residents of Uttar Pradesh: You guys have your own set of rules. Check your state election website for the nitty-gritty.

Essential Documents to Apply for Voter ID Online and Offline
After checking off the eligibility criteria, the next step is to submit your documents. A selfie will not suffice, as you will need to provide 
proper paperwork to verify your identity.

The Must-Haves
Proof of Age: Birth certificate or 10th-grade mark sheet.

Proof of Address: Utility bill or bank passbook indicating permanent address.

Proof of Identity: Aadhaar, PAN card, or other official documents.

Photograph: A passport-sized photograph is required.

Verification Steps after You Apply for Voter ID Card Online or Offline

After submitting your vote, the verification process begins. The Election Commission of India acts as a detective to confirm your identity.


Online Verification

If you have applied online, you will receive an email or SMS instructing you to confirm your details. By clicking the link and following 
the simple steps, you will be verified. This process is carried out by the Election Commission to ensure the accuracy of your application
form and relevant information.


Offline Verification

If you choose to vote offline, be prepared for a visit by a Booth Level Officer (BLO) who will verify your original documents and possibly 
ask some questions. It is similar to a pop quiz, but for the purpose of ensuring a fair democratic process.

Whether you've applied online or offline, you'll need to wait for your name to appear in the voter list. This is the final stamp of approval.
You can check the status of your voter application by visiting the Voter Portal or using the Voter Helpline Mobile Application.


Things to Remember Before a Voter ID Application

Obtaining a Voter ID in India is essential for exercising democratic rights, requiring applicants to be 18 or older, mentally sound, and 
without criminal or financial issues. Form 6 must be filled out accurately through the NVSP website or approved centres, with correct 
personal details. Verification of provided information and documents is crucial upon receiving the ID. Ensuring accuracy and legality in 
the process maintains the integrity of voter registration, fostering a fair democratic system in India.


Voter ID Verification

To confirm the authenticity of their Voter ID, applicants can utilise their Voter ID number. They should begin by verifying if their names
are listed in the electoral roll and then cross-check the details provided. Any inconsistencies should be promptly reported to the Chief 
Electoral Officer for resolution. 

Here are the steps to verify a Voter ID:

Go to the National Voters' Services Portal.

Navigate to the 'Search in Electoral Roll' section.

Choose one of the search options: 'Search by Details', 'Search by EPIC', or 'Search by Mobile'.

Input the required information and initiate the search.

Your details will be shown on the screen, and you can verify their accuracy by selecting 'View Details' under the 'Action' column.

Following these steps enables individuals to ensure the legitimacy of their Voter ID, contributing to the integrity of the electoral process.


Steps to Take In Case You Don't Get Voter ID

If your Voter ID hasn't arrived yet, don't panic. Follow these steps:

Log in to the NVSP portal.

Access 'Track Application Status'.

Enter your reference number from the form submission, choose your state, and click 'Submit'.

Check the updated status of your application.

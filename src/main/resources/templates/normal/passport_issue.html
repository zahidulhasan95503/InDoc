Common Issues

The most common hurdle is police not visiting, or finding that the address provided does not match records, or the applicant is not present at the given address.

How to fix:

  • Be Present: Ensure you or a reliable family member is present at the given address during the verification period.
  • Prepare Documents: Keep your original ID, address proof, and 2 references from your locality ready for the visiting officer.
  • Track Verification: Check the exact status of your police verification online at the Track Application Status portal.
  • Visit Police Station / RPO: If the verification is excessively delayed or failed, visit your local police station or book an enquiry appointment at your Regional Passport Office (RPO).

Mismatched names, incorrect dates of birth, or unclear/unattested photocopies of documents lead to delays or rejections at the PSK.

How to fix:

  • Check Consistency: Cross-verify your exact name spelling and DOB across all documents (Aadhaar, PAN, Marksheets) before applying. They must match perfectly.
  • Use Document Advisor: Use the official Document Advisor to generate an exact list of valid documents required for your specific case.
  • Self-Attestation: Ensure all photocopies you carry to the PSK are clear, legible, and signed (self-attested) by you. Carry originals for all copies.

Failure to attach specific declarations (Annexure) for cases like minor applicants, lost passports, or changes in personal details can put applications on hold.

How to fix:

  • Identify if your application falls into a special category (e.g., Minor applying without one parent, Tatkal, Lost/Damaged Passport).
  • Download the correct and latest standard affidavit formats from the "Forms and Affidavits" section on the Passport Seva Portal.
  • Ensure the annexure is printed on plain paper (or non-judicial stamp paper if specified), properly filled, signed, and notarized if explicitly required.

Applicants fail to track their applications, leading to missed opportunities to rectify issues highlighted by the Passport Seva Kendra (PSK).

How to fix:

  • Track Regularly: Enter your 15-digit alphanumeric File Number and Date of Birth at the Application Status Tracking Page.
  • Respond to Clarifications: If the status says a document or clarification is pending, book an "Enquiry Appointment" at your RPO immediately to provide the missing information.
  • SMS Alerts: Subscribe to SMS alerts during your PSK visit for real-time updates directly to your mobile number.

Issues arising if an applicant has changed residence and failed to provide proof of the new address.

How to fix:

  • You must apply with the address where you currently reside, not your permanent address (if they differ).
  • Provide valid proof of your current address, such as an Aadhaar card updated with the new address, a registered rent agreement, or a recent utility bill in your name.
  • If you moved immediately before applying, keep proof of your previous address handy in case the PSK or police officer requests it.

Inconsistent signatures across documents or poor-quality photos can cause complications, especially during the biometric capture stage at the PSK.

How to fix:

  • Sign Consistently: Maintain a standard signature across all forms, self-attested copies, and the digital pad at the PSK.
  • Photos at PSK: Remember that your primary passport photo is taken live by the camera at the PSK. Wear dark-colored clothing (to contrast with the white background) and avoid heavy makeup or large accessories.
  • Minors & Infants: For children below 4 years, you may need to carry a recent passport-sized photograph with a white background as live capture can be difficult.

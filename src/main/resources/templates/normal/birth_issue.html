Common Issues

Frequent mistakes in the child’s name, parents’ names, or date of birth, leading to discrepancies with official IDs.

How to fix:

  • Minor Corrections: Apply for a correction at the local municipal corporation or registrar's office where the birth was registered. Submit an affidavit, parents' original IDs, and hospital discharge summary.
  • Major Name Change: If the name differs entirely, you may need to publish a Gazette Notification and submit it to the registrar.
  • Online Application: In some states, corrections can be initiated via the CRS Portal or specific state e-District portals.

Older or incomplete records often omit the child's name ("Missing Name of the Child"), necessitating a formal name addition process.

How to fix:

  • Time Limit: A child's name can be added to the birth registry free of cost within 1 year of birth. After 1 year, it can be added up to 15 years with a late fee.
  • Process: Parents must visit the municipal/registrar office with the original unnamed birth certificate and their identity proofs (Aadhaar, Voter ID).
  • Details: Learn more about name addition policies on the official Civil Registration System (CRS) website.

Mistakes in recording the hospital or municipality, causing issues for passport/visa applications or domicile proofs.

How to fix:

  • Hospital Letter: Obtain a certified letter or discharge summary from the actual hospital confirming the correct place and date of birth.
  • Registrar Visit: Submit this letter along with a sworn affidavit to the local Registrar of Births and Deaths. Physical verification may be conducted before a corrected certificate is issued.

Inefficiency in local, state, or municipal offices results in prolonged waiting periods to receive the final certificate.

How to fix:

  • Track Online: If you applied through an e-District or CRS portal, regularly track your application reference number online.
  • File a Grievance: If the delay is unreasonable, you can lodge a grievance with the state government or use the central CPGRAMS Portal for administrative delays.
  • RTI Application: Filing a Right to Information (RTI) request often expedites delayed government applications by holding officials accountable.

If not registered within 21 days, the process requires an affidavit, police verification, and approval from the Area Magistrate (SDM), often causing delays.

How to fix:

  • 21 to 30 Days: Can be registered with written permission of the Registrar and a nominal late fee.
  • 30 Days to 1 Year: Requires an affidavit made before a notary public and written permission from the Chief Medical Officer/Prescribed Authority.
  • Beyond 1 Year: Mandatory order from a First Class Magistrate or SDM is required. You must first obtain a Non-Availability Certificate (NAC) from the municipal office.
  • For detailed rules, refer to the Registration of Births and Deaths Act.

Birth certificates often mismatch with school certificates, Aadhar cards, or PAN cards, requiring difficult corrections.

How to fix:

  • Update other documents: Generally, a birth certificate is the foundational document. Instead of changing it, you may update your Aadhaar Card or PAN Card to match the birth certificate.
  • Birth Certificate Correction: If the birth certificate itself is genuinely incorrect, gather secondary proof (10th Board mark sheet, Passport, notarized affidavit) and submit an application for correction to the municipal authority.

In cases of lost records, the applicant is required to provide extensive evidence, such as school leaving certificates or municipal records, which may not be available.

How to fix:

  • Non-Availability Certificate (NAC): If the registration is not found, apply for an NAC at the local municipal office. This acts as proof that the record does not currently exist.
  • Alternate Proofs: Use the NAC along with secondary evidence (School Leaving Certificate, vaccination records, parent’s affidavit, hospital certificates) to formally apply for a fresh registration via a Magistrate order.
  • Check if your state offers digital retrieval via DigiLocker or the National Portal (services.india.gov.in).

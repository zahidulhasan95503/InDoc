Common Issues

Sometimes the name on your voter ID does not match Aadhaar, PAN, or other IDs due to typing mistakes.

How to fix:

  • Submit Form 8: This can be corrected by submitting Form 8 online along with valid proof of identity.

Tip: Always check spellings carefully before submitting.

If you have moved to a new house or city, your old address may still appear on your voter ID.

How to fix:

  • Within same constituency: Use Form 8 online (shifting of residence within the same constituency).
  • New city or constituency: Apply afresh in the new location using Form 6 and request deletion of the old record.

Errors in age or date of birth can cause disputes about eligibility.

How to fix:

  • To fix this, submit Form 8 online with proof such as a birth certificate, Aadhaar, or school certificate.

Duplicate entries often occur when people reapply instead of updating.

How to fix:

  • To fix this, submit Form 7 online to delete the older record before updating your details.

Applications sometimes stay pending due to missing documents or portal errors.

How to fix:

  • Track Status: You can track the status on the Voter Helpline portal or State Election Commission website.
  • Visit ERO: Visit the local Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) with your acknowledgment slip.

Tip: If online submissions fail, try another browser, avoid peak hours, or opt for offline submission.

Unique Mobile Number Rule: To download the e-EPIC, your mobile number must be uniquely linked to your Voter ID.

If multiple family members used the same number for their applications, the OTP system for digital downloads often fails.

How to fix:

  • Update Mobile Number: Submit Form 8 online to update and link a unique mobile number to your Voter ID. Using the Voter Helpline App is also recommended.

How to Apply for Aadhar Online & Offline

The Aadhaar card is one of the primary ID proofs for all the citizens of India. Over the past decade, this has become one of the most important documents for Indians and is required for most processes, both online and offline.

The Aadhaar card contains a 12-digit unique identification number along with personal details like name, address, date of birth, and gender. The biometric and demographic data is recorded and stored during enrollment.

Important to Know
  • The entire Aadhar enrollment process is FREE of cost
  • You must personally visit an Aadhar Seva Kendra for biometric capture (Photo, Fingerprints, Iris)
  • Aadhar card will be delivered to your address within 90 days of enrollment
  • You can track your application using the EID (Enrollment ID) number

Documents Required for Aadhaar Enrollment

Note: You need to provide documents for Proof of Identity (POI), Proof of Address (POA), and Proof of Date of Birth (POB). Some documents can serve multiple purposes.
Proof of Identity (Name & Photo)
  • Passport
  • P AN Card
  • Voter ID / Election Photo Identity Card
  • Driving License
  • Government Photo ID Cards
  • Service Photo ID issued by PSU
  • Bank Passbook with Photo
  • Pensioner Photo Card
  • Freedom Fighter Photo Card
  • NREGS Job Card
Proof of Address
  • Passport
  • Bank Statement / Passbook
  • Post Office Account Statement
  • Ration Card
  • Voter ID
  • Driving License
  • Electricity Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Water Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Telephone Bill (not older than 3 months)
  • Property Tax Receipt
  • Rent Agreement
Proof of Date of Birth
  • Birth Certificate
  • Passport
  • PAN Card
  • School/College Certificate (10th/12th)
  • Matriculation Certificate
  • Pension Payment Order
  • Domicile Certificate
For Children (Below 5 years)
  • Birth Certificate
  • Hospital Discharge Certificate
  • Parent's Aadhaar (POI)
  • Documents showing relationship with parents

Note: Biometrics not required for children under 5

Method 1: Apply for Aadhar Online (Booking Appointment)

You can book an online appointment to avoid long queues at Aadhar centers. This method allows you to schedule a convenient time slot for enrollment.

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Visit the Official UIDAI Portal
    Go to appointments.uidai.gov.in or uidai.gov.in
  2. Click on "Book an Appointment"
    Select the option for "New Enrolment" or "Aadhaar Enrolment"
  3. Enter Mobile Number
    Provide your 10-digit mobile number and enter the captcha code displayed
  4. OTP Verification
    Click "Generate OTP" → You'll receive a 6-digit OTP on your mobile
    Enter the OTP and click "Verify OTP"
  5. Select Enrollment Type
    Choose "Resident" (for Indian citizens)
    Select "Application Verification Type" as "Document"
  6. Choose Location
    Select your State from the dropdown
    Select your District/City
    Choose the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra from the list
  7. Fill Personal Details
    Enter the following information accurately:
    • Full Name (as per documents)
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth
    • Complete Address with Pin Code
    • Select Address Proof document type
  8. Select Appointment Date & Time
    Choose a convenient date (available slots will be shown)
    Select your preferred time slot
  9. Review and Submit
    Carefully review all details entered
    Click "Submit" to confirm your appointment
  10. Confirmation
    You'll receive an SMS confirmation with appointment details
    Note down your Appointment Reference Number
On Appointment Day: Visit the Aadhar Seva Kendra 10-15 minutes before your scheduled time with all original documents.

Method 2: Apply for Aadhar Offline (Direct Visit)

You can directly visit any Aadhaar Enrollment Center without prior appointment. This method is suitable if you prefer walk-in service or if online booking is not available.

Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Locate Nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra
    Use the Center Locator on UIDAI website
    You can find centers at post offices, banks, government offices, and dedicated Aadhaar centers
  2. Prepare Required Documents
    Carry original documents + 1 photocopy of each (see document list below)
    Ensure documents clearly show your name, address, and date of birth
  3. Visit the Center
    Visit during working hours (usually 9 AM - 5 PM, Monday to Saturday)
    Be prepared for potential waiting time during peak hours
  4. Get Token Number
    Collect a token/queue number from the reception
    Wait for your turn to be called
  5. Fill Enrollment Form
    The operator will help you fill the Aadhaar enrollment form (Form A)
    Provide accurate information as per your documents
  6. Submit Documents
    Submit your original documents for verification
    The operator will verify and attach photocopies to your application
  7. Biometric Capture
    Photograph: A digital photo will be captured
    Fingerprints: All 10 fingerprints will be scanned
    Iris Scan: Both eyes will be scanned for iris biometrics
  8. Verify Details
    Review all entered information on the screen
    Confirm accuracy before final submission
  9. Collect Acknowledgment Slip
    You'll receive an acknowledgment slip with:
    • EID (Enrollment ID) - 14-digit number
    • Date and time of enrollment
    • Center details
    KEEP THIS SLIP SAFE! You'll need the EID to track your application
Processing Time: Your Aadhaar card will be generated and sent to your registered address within 60-90 days.
